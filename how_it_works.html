Basic Functions and User Interface

Install the app onto your smart phone or tablet. At the first launch, the app will take you to the "Settings" tab. You can choose to enter personal info, which will go to the Allegheny County Health Department (ACHD) with every smell report you submit, so that they can follow up with you if needed. The "Settings" tab also lets you choose the type(s) of app notifications you want.

When you experience a pollution odor outdoors, launch the app and pick a rating for the smell (from 1 through 5). Adding a description of the smell (e.g. industrial) is highly encouraged, since this can help the county isolate the source. You also have the option to (but are not required to) list symptoms attributable to the experience and send a personal note/question to ACHD.

Please only enter information that you are comfortable with sharing anonymously on the public Smell PGH map. Once the information is entered, submit the report. The information you report is then pulled together in an email format that includes the location (based on the GPS location of your phone), date and time, rating and description (if provided), and personal notes and/or questions to ACHD (if provided). This email is then sent directly to ACHD.

At the same time your smell report is added to the public Smell PGH map. A triangle icon shows the color of your smell rating that represents your report. Your location is obscured on the map by slightly shifting the coordinates to protect your privacy. Tapping a smell report icon will show the details of the report, including the: date, time, rating, description (if provided), and symptoms (if provided). NO personally identifiable information is shown on the map.

In addition, circles on the map indicate government-operated air quality monitoring sensor stations, where the color denotes the level of fine particulate readings. Blue arrows which appear near the circles denote the wind directions as reported from that monitoring stations. You can explore this map further by panning and zooming. You can also click on the playback button to animate the smell reports over time.

The bottom of the visualization features a timeline with squares, where you can select different dates. The gray level of the square indicates the number of smell reports which have ratings larger or equal than 3 in the corresponding date. So darker colors indicate a higher number of foul odor reports.
